About FromSoftware

FromSoftware, Inc. is a Japanese video game development company founded in November 1986 by Naotoshi Zin. The company is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, and is currently led by president and game director Hidetaka Miyazaki, who joined the company in 2004.

The company gained widespread recognition for their challenging gameplay, intricate level design, and deep lore that often requires player interpretation. Their games are known for their difficulty, atmospheric worlds, and minimalist storytelling approach.

FromSoftware has evolved from a developer of utility software to one of the most influential game studios in the industry, with their titles defining the "Soulslike" subgenre of action RPGs.

Company History

Early Years (1986-2004)

FromSoftware initially developed business software before transitioning to video games in 1994 with the release of King's Field for the PlayStation. During this period, they created several series including Armored Core, Echo Night, and Tenchu.

Miyazaki Era (2004-Present)

The company underwent a significant transformation after Hidetaka Miyazaki joined and eventually became president in 2014. Under his leadership, FromSoftware developed the critically acclaimed Souls series, Bloodborne, Sekiro, and Elden Ring, establishing themselves as masters of challenging action RPGs.

Kadokawa Acquisition

In 2014, FromSoftware was acquired by Japanese media conglomerate Kadokawa Corporation, becoming a subsidiary while maintaining creative independence.

Souls Series

Demon's Souls (2009)

The game that started it all. Demon's Souls was released exclusively for PlayStation 3 and later received a remake for PlayStation 5 developed by Bluepoint Games in 2020.

Story

Set in the kingdom of Boletaria, which has been consumed by a colorless fog that brings demons who feed on human souls. Players take on the role of a hero who enters the fog to kill the demons and free Boletaria from its plight.

Key Features

  • Five interconnected worlds accessed through the Nexus hub area
  • Introduced the challenging gameplay that would define FromSoftware
  • Featured unique online mechanics including player messages and phantoms
  • World Tendency system that changes the difficulty and reveals secrets
  • Directed by Hidetaka Miyazaki, who was relatively unknown at the time

Legacy

Initially considered a niche title, Demon's Souls developed a cult following that led to the creation of Dark Souls and established many of the mechanics that would become staples of the genre.

Dark Souls (2011)

The spiritual successor to Demon's Souls that expanded on its formula and gained a larger audience. Released for PlayStation 3, Xbox 360, and PC, with a remastered version released in 2018.

Story

Set in the kingdom of Lordran, players take on the role of the Chosen Undead who must decide whether to link the flame and prolong the Age of Fire or let it fade and usher in the Age of Dark.

Key Features

  • Masterfully interconnected world design with shortcuts and secrets
  • Deep lore centered around the Age of Fire and the gods of Lordran
  • Introduced the Bonfire system as checkpoints
  • Refined combat mechanics from Demon's Souls
  • Memorable boss fights including Ornstein and Smough, Artorias, and Gwyn
  • Expanded covenant system for multiplayer alignment

DLC: Artorias of the Abyss

This expansion added new areas, bosses, and equipment while exploring the backstory of Knight Artorias, one of the most beloved characters in the series.

Dark Souls II (2014)

The second entry in the Dark Souls series, directed by Tomohiro Shibuya and Yui Tanimura rather than Miyazaki, who was working on Bloodborne at the time.

Story

Set in the kingdom of Drangleic, players take on the role of a cursed undead seeking a cure for their affliction. The game explores themes of memory and the cyclical nature of the world.

Key Features

  • Expanded character customization and build variety
  • Introduced power stancing for dual-wielding weapons
  • Life gems as supplementary healing items
  • Torch mechanics for dark areas
  • Bonfire Ascetics to increase area difficulty
  • Enhanced New Game+ with additional enemies and items

DLC Trilogy

Dark Souls II received three DLC expansions: Crown of the Sunken King, Crown of the Old Iron King, and Crown of the Ivory King, collectively known as The Lost Crowns trilogy. These expansions are often considered the best content in the game.

Scholar of the First Sin

An enhanced edition released in 2015 that included all DLC, improved graphics, and reworked enemy placements.

Dark Souls III (2016)

The final entry in the Dark Souls series, bringing the story of the Age of Fire to a conclusion. Directed by Hidetaka Miyazaki, returning to the series after Bloodborne.

Story

Set in the kingdom of Lothric, players take on the role of an Unkindled, an undead who failed to link the flame in the past. The game explores the end of the cycle as the fire fades and the world approaches its conclusion.

Key Features

  • Faster combat inspired by Bloodborne
  • Weapon Arts system for special attacks
  • FP (Focus Points) bar for magic and special abilities
  • Linear world design with interconnected areas
  • Featured references and callbacks to previous games in the series
  • Refined multiplayer with dedicated servers

DLC

Ashes of Ariandel introduced a new painted world and PvP arena, while The Ringed City provided a fitting conclusion to the series with challenging areas and bosses that tied together many narrative threads.

Bloodborne (2015)

A PlayStation 4 exclusive that took the Souls formula in a new direction with a Gothic, Lovecraftian setting. Directed by Hidetaka Miyazaki.

Story

Set in the city of Yharnam, known for its medical blood treatments. Players take on the role of a Hunter who arrives seeking a cure for an affliction, only to find the city overrun by beasts. As the night progresses, the game transitions from Gothic horror to cosmic horror.

Key Features

  • Faster, more aggressive combat that rewards offensive play
  • Rally system allowing players to regain lost health by attacking
  • Unique trick weapons that can transform between two forms
  • Firearms used for parrying instead of shields
  • Cosmic horror themes inspired by H.P. Lovecraft
  • Insight mechanic that changes how players perceive the world
  • Chalice Dungeons offering procedurally generated content

DLC: The Old Hunters

This expansion added new areas, weapons, and bosses while exploring the history of the first hunters and the origins of the nightmare.

Legacy

Bloodborne is often cited as one of the greatest games of all time and has developed a dedicated fanbase who continue to uncover secrets and lore years after release.

Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice (2019)

A departure from the Souls formula, focusing on a single character with a fixed playstyle and a greater emphasis on stealth and precise combat. Released for PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and PC.

Story

Set in a fictionalized version of Sengoku period Japan, players control Wolf, a shinobi serving a young lord who is kidnapped. The game explores themes of immortality, duty, and the consequences of seeking power.

Key Features

  • Posture system that focuses on breaking an opponent's guard rather than depleting health
  • Deflection-based combat requiring precise timing
  • Resurrection mechanic allowing players to revive after death
  • Vertical traversal with a grappling hook
  • Prosthetic arm tools that can be upgraded and modified
  • Stealth mechanics allowing for alternative approaches
  • Multiple endings based on player choices
  • No multiplayer components, focusing entirely on single-player

Recognition

Sekiro won Game of the Year at The Game Awards 2019 and was praised for its combat system and world design.

Free Update: Reflections and Gauntlets

A post-launch update added boss rush modes, costume changes, and recording features.

Elden Ring (2022)

An open-world action RPG developed in collaboration with fantasy author George R.R. Martin, who contributed to the world-building and mythology. Released for PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X/S, and PC.

Story

Set in the Lands Between, a realm ruled by demigods who each possess a shard of the shattered Elden Ring. Players take on the role of a Tarnished, exiles who lost the grace of the Erdtree, who return to the realm to restore the Elden Ring and become Elden Lord.

Key Features

  • Vast open world with six major regions and numerous dungeons, catacombs, and caves
  • Legacy Dungeons that feature more traditional Souls-like level design
  • Torrent, a spectral steed for mounted exploration and combat
  • Spirit Ashes that summon AI allies in battle
  • Expanded combat options including guard counters and jump attacks
  • Crafting system for creating items
  • Weather and day/night cycle affecting gameplay
  • Over 100 bosses throughout the world
  • Multiple endings based on questlines completed

DLC: Shadow of the Erdtree

A major expansion released in 2024 that added a new region, the Land of Shadow, with new weapons, armor, and bosses.

Recognition

Elden Ring won Game of the Year at The Game Awards 2022 and is FromSoftware's best-selling title, with over 20 million copies sold worldwide.

Armored Core Series

Before the Souls series, FromSoftware was known for the Armored Core series of mecha combat games, which began in 1997 and spans multiple generations of consoles.

Series Overview

The Armored Core series focuses on players piloting and customizing giant mechs called Armored Cores. The games typically feature mission-based structures with an emphasis on mech customization and combat.

Armored Core VI: Fires of Rubicon (2023)

The latest entry in the long-running mecha series, returning after a decade-long hiatus. Released for PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X/S, and PC.

Story

Set on the planet Rubicon 3, which was devastated by a catastrophe involving a new energy source called Coral. Players take on the role of a mercenary piloting an Armored Core in a war between corporations fighting for control of the planet's resources.

Key Features

  • Highly customizable mechs with hundreds of parts
  • Fast-paced, three-dimensional combat
  • Mission-based structure with rankings
  • Multiple endings based on choices made
  • Arena mode for testing builds against AI opponents
  • Combines elements from previous Armored Core games with lessons learned from the Souls series
  • Challenging boss fights requiring strategy and adaptation

Reception

Armored Core VI was praised for successfully reviving the series while incorporating modern game design elements and maintaining the core identity of Armored Core.

Other Notable FromSoftware Games

King's Field Series (1994-2001)

FromSoftware's first game series, consisting of first-person RPGs that are considered precursors to the Souls series in terms of atmosphere and difficulty.

Otogi Series (2002-2003)

Action games set in feudal Japan with destructible environments and mythological themes.

Chromehounds (2006)

A mech combat game with an emphasis on team-based multiplayer and strategic gameplay.

3D Dot Game Heroes (2009)

A homage to classic 8-bit adventure games, particularly The Legend of Zelda, featuring voxel-based graphics.

Déraciné (2018)

A VR adventure game set in a boarding school where players take on the role of a faerie interacting with the students.

Signature Gameplay Elements

Challenging Difficulty

FromSoftware games are known for their high difficulty that demands patience, observation, and skill. Death is frequent but serves as a learning tool rather than a punishment.

Stamina Management

Most actions in combat consume stamina, requiring players to carefully manage their resources and not overcommit to attacks.

Deliberate Combat

Combat is methodical and weighty, with an emphasis on timing, positioning, and understanding enemy patterns rather than button mashing.

Risk vs. Reward

Games often present players with choices between safer, slower progress or riskier approaches with potentially greater rewards.

Loss on Death

When players die, they typically lose their accumulated currency (souls, blood echoes, etc.) and must retrieve it from where they died or lose it permanently.

Environmental Storytelling

Much of the narrative is conveyed through the environment, item descriptions, and NPC dialogue rather than cutscenes or exposition.

Storytelling & Lore

FromSoftware's approach to storytelling is distinctive, often described as minimalist or environmental. Rather than presenting the narrative through lengthy cutscenes or exposition, the games reveal their stories through:

  • Item descriptions that provide historical context and character backgrounds
  • Environmental details that tell stories about what happened in locations
  • Cryptic NPC dialogue that requires interpretation
  • Enemy and boss designs that reflect their roles in the world
  • Architecture and world design that conveys the history and culture of the setting

This approach encourages community engagement as players work together to piece together the narrative and develop theories about the world and its characters.

Common Themes

  • Cycles of decay and renewal
  • The corrupting influence of power
  • The consequences of immortality
  • The relationship between humanity and divinity
  • The nature of free will versus destiny

Music & Sound Design

FromSoftware games are renowned for their atmospheric music and meticulous sound design, which contribute significantly to the immersive experience.

Notable Composers

  • Motoi Sakuraba (Dark Souls series)
  • Yuka Kitamura (Dark Souls II, III, Bloodborne, Sekiro, Elden Ring)
  • Tsukasa Saitoh (Bloodborne, Sekiro, Elden Ring)
  • Shinsuke Kida (Demon's Souls)
  • Nobuyoshi Suzuki (Dark Souls III, Elden Ring)
  • Tai Tomisawa (Armored Core VI)

Sound Design Characteristics

  • Sparse use of music, often reserved for boss fights and key locations
  • Ambient environmental sounds that enhance atmosphere
  • Distinctive sound effects for different weapons and attacks
  • Audio cues that provide gameplay information

Iconic Tracks

  • "Gwyn, Lord of Cinder" (Dark Souls)
  • "Ludwig, the Holy Blade" (Bloodborne)
  • "Slave Knight Gael" (Dark Souls III)
  • "Elden Ring Main Theme" (Elden Ring)
  • "Cleric Beast" (Bloodborne)

Cultural Impact

FromSoftware's games, particularly the Souls series, have had a profound impact on the gaming industry and culture:

Creation of a Subgenre

The "Soulslike" has become a recognized subgenre of action RPGs, with numerous games inspired by FromSoftware's design philosophy.

Influence on Game Design

Many developers have incorporated elements of FromSoftware's approach to difficulty, level design, and storytelling into their own games.

Community and Content Creation

FromSoftware games have inspired dedicated communities that create lore videos, challenge runs, mods, and fan art.

"Git Gud" Culture

The phrase "git gud" (get good) emerged from the community as both a challenge and encouragement to improve through practice rather than seeking easier alternatives.

Accessibility Debates

FromSoftware's games have sparked ongoing discussions about difficulty, accessibility, and the artistic vision of game developers.
